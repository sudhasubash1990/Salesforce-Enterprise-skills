# Vision

## Why This Repository Exists

Enterprise Salesforce programs invest heavily in licenses, integrators, and internal teams—yet many still struggle with rework, scope creep, and user adoption failures. The root cause is rarely "Salesforce can't do it." More often, it is:

- Requirements that describe screens instead of outcomes
- Backlogs that mix epics, tasks, and stories without hierarchy
- Fit-gap decisions made too late or without platform literacy
- UAT that tests clicks instead of business scenarios

This repository encodes **how senior practitioners prevent those failures**—in a form that both humans and AI agents can execute consistently.

## Strategic Vision

**By 2027**, Salesforce Enterprise Skills will be the default knowledge layer for Salesforce consulting disciplines inside AI-assisted delivery—starting with Business Analysis and expanding to architecture, testing, and change management.

## Principles

### 1. Outcome-Oriented Requirements

Every requirement answers: *What business outcome improves, for whom, and how do we know it succeeded?*

### 2. Platform Literacy

Business analysts must understand what Salesforce does natively. The best BA on a Salesforce program is part translator, part product owner, part solution advisor.

### 3. Traceability as a First-Class Concern

Requirement → User Story → Test Scenario → Release Note. No orphan artifacts.

### 4. Industry Context Without Lock-In

Patterns from Financial Services, Healthcare, Manufacturing, and Public Sector inform defaults—but every engagement validates against client-specific constraints.

### 5. Agent-Ready, Human-Accountable

AI accelerates drafting; humans approve. All agent outputs follow explicit quality gates and assumption labeling.

## Success Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Requirement rework rate in pilot programs | ↓ 30% vs. baseline |
| User story acceptance criteria completeness | ≥ 95% pass INVEST + checklist |
| Time to first approved BRD section | ↓ 40% with agent assist |
| Practitioner satisfaction (survey) | ≥ 4.2 / 5 |

## What We Are Not

- A replacement for Salesforce official documentation
- A single client's methodology frozen in time
- A code library or metadata deployment package

## North Star

A practitioner—or an agent guided by this repository—can walk into any enterprise Salesforce engagement and produce **clear, traceable, platform-aware requirements** that accelerate delivery and reduce regret.
