# Glossary

Salesforce and enterprise BA terminology used consistently across this repository.

## A

**Acceptance Criteria** — Conditions that must be true for a user story to be considered complete. Must be testable.

**Account** — Standard Salesforce object representing a company or individual customer.

**ADR (Architecture Decision Record)** — Document capturing a significant technical or solution decision with context and consequences.

**AS-IS** — Current state process, system, or capability before transformation.

## B

**Backlog** — Prioritized list of epics, stories, and tasks. Single source of truth for delivery scope.

**BRD (Business Requirements Document)** — Authoritative document describing business needs, scope, and requirements.

**Business Capability** — What the organization does, independent of system implementation.

## C

**Capability Map** — Structured view of business capabilities and supporting applications. See `salesforce-capability-map.md`.

**Change Request** — Formal proposal to alter approved scope, timeline, or cost.

**Contact** — Standard Salesforce object representing a person associated with an Account.

## D

**Discovery** — Structured elicitation phase to understand problem, stakeholders, and constraints.

**Done** — Team-defined completion criteria beyond individual story acceptance.

## E

**Epic** — Large body of work decomposed into user stories; spans multiple sprints.

**Experience Cloud** — Salesforce digital experience platform (communities, portals).

## F

**Fit-Gap** — Analysis comparing business needs against platform standard capabilities.

**Flow** — Salesforce declarative automation (Record-Triggered, Screen, Scheduled).

## G

**Governor Limits** — Platform execution limits affecting design (queries, DML, CPU).

## I

**INVEST** — Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable (user story quality).

## L

**Lead** — Standard object for prospective customers before qualification.

**Lightning Web Component (LWC)** — Modern UI framework for Salesforce.

## M

**MVP (Minimum Viable Product)** — Smallest release delivering measurable business value.

## O

**Opportunity** — Standard object for qualified sales deals.

**Org** — Salesforce instance (production or sandbox).

## P

**Person Account** — Account model treating individuals as accounts (B2C pattern).

**Process Builder** — Legacy automation; migrate to Flow in new designs.

## R

**Record Type** — Mechanism to vary page layouts, picklists, and processes by segment.

**RACI** — Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed — stakeholder responsibility matrix.

## S

**Sharing Model** — Org-wide defaults, role hierarchy, sharing rules controlling record access.

**Sprint** — Time-boxed iteration in Agile delivery.

## T

**TO-BE** — Future state process or capability after transformation.

**Traceability Matrix** — Mapping requirements to stories, design, and tests.

## U

**UAT (User Acceptance Testing)** — Business validation that solution meets requirements.

**User Story** — Short statement of value: As a [role], I want [goal], so that [benefit].

## W

**Workshop** — Facilitated session for elicitation, design, or validation.
