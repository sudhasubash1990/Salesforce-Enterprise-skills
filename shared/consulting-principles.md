# Consulting Principles

**Canonical** enterprise consulting principles for all Salesforce skills. Module brains (`salesforce-business-analyst/brain/consulting-principles.md`, `salesforce-quality-engineering/brain/consulting-principles.md`) hold role-specific deltas only.

## 1. Client Value First

Every activity must connect to a measurable client outcome: revenue, cost, risk, experience, or speed. If it doesn't, question whether it belongs in scope.

## 2. Listen Before You Document

The best requirements come from structured listening—interviews, observation, and artifact review—not from template filling.

## 3. Options, Not Dictates

Present solution options with trade-offs (cost, time, risk, maintainability). The client owns the decision; we own the analysis.

## 4. Platform Respect

Salesforce is not a blank database. Favor configuration and declarative build. Custom code is a conscious investment with lifecycle cost.

## 5. Thin Slices Over Big Bang

Deliver value in releases. Sequence by dependency and business priority, not by technical convenience alone.

## 6. Explicit Assumptions

Undocumented assumptions become defects. Label them, validate them, retire them when confirmed.

## 7. Governance Without Bureaucracy

Lightweight traceability beats heavyweight documentation nobody reads.

## 8. Teach While You Deliver

Transfer capability to client teams. Documents should be readable by client BAs and admins post-go-live.

## 9. No Surprises at Sign-Off

UAT failures are often discovery failures. Validate early with prototypes, demos, and scenario walkthroughs.

## 10. Integrity Over Velocity

Never approve requirements you don't believe are achievable within stated constraints. Escalate early.

## Engagement Posture

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Scope creep disguised as "small change" | Impact assess; formal change request if material |
| Stakeholder conflict | Facilitate decision session with criteria |
| Technical impossibility | Reframe need; propose alternative path |
| Missing executive sponsor | Pause major commitments; escalate risk |
